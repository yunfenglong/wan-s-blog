CNF Construction Patterns

Here are systematic ways to express constraints like "pick at least k" or "pick at most k" in CNF. Let your set of choices be S={x1,x2,…,xn}S = \{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n\}S={x1​,x2​,…,xn​}.

1. Pick at least kkk from a set of nnn options

To ensure at least kkk items are chosen, you must prevent the case where you pick too few. This means that out of any group of n−k+1n-k+1n−k+1 items, at least one must be chosen.

  • Clause Size: Each clause will have n−k+1n-k+1n−k+1 literals.
  • Number of Clauses: (nn−k+1)\binom{n}{n-k+1}(n−k+1n​)
  • Example: Pick at least 2 from {a,b,c}\{a, b, c\}{a,b,c}. (n=3,k=2n=3, k=2n=3,k=2)
    • Out of any group of n−k+1=3−2+1=2n-k+1 = 3-2+1 = 2n−k+1=3−2+1=2 items, at least one must be chosen.
    • The clauses are: (a∨b)∧(a∨c)∧(b∨c)(a \lor b) \land (a \lor c) \land (b \lor c)(a∨b)∧(a∨c)∧(b∨c).

2. Pick at most kkk from a set of nnn options

To ensure at most kkk items are chosen, you must prevent the case where you pick too many. This is equivalent to saying you cannot pick at least k+1k+1k+1 items. This means that out of any group of k+1k+1k+1 items, at least one must not be chosen (i.e., must be false).

  • Clause Size: Each clause will have k+1k+1k+1 negated literals.
  • Number of Clauses: (nk+1)\binom{n}{k+1}(k+1n​)
  • Example: Pick at most 2 from {a,b,c,d}\{a, b, c, d\}{a,b,c,d}. (n=4,k=2n=4, k=2n=4,k=2)
    • Out of any group of k+1=3k+1 = 3k+1=3 items, at least one must be false.
    • The clauses are: (¬a∨¬b∨¬c)∧(¬a∨¬b∨¬d)∧(¬a∨¬c∨¬d)∧(¬b∨¬c∨¬d)(\neg a \lor \neg b \lor \neg c) \land (\neg a \lor \neg b \lor \neg d) \land (\neg a \lor \neg c \lor \neg d) \land (\neg b \lor \neg c \lor \neg d)(¬a∨¬b∨¬c)∧(¬a∨¬b∨¬d)∧(¬a∨¬c∨¬d)∧(¬b∨¬c∨¬d).

3. Pick exactly kkk from a set of nnn options

To get exactly kkk, you simply combine the previous two constraints with an AND: (Pick at least kkk) ∧\land∧ (Pick at most kkk)

Enhancement

if you want to pick at least k items from a set of size n:

  • write down n symbols
  • draw a circle around the first k
  • draw a box that starts from the last symbol, and excloses exactly one circled symbol
  • the size of your box gives the size of each of your clauses
